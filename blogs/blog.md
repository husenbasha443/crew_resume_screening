**
Based on the job requirements and candidate skills summary, here are some relevant interview questions for the Software Development Manager position:

**Technical Questions:**

1. Can you explain the differences between Scrum and Kanban methodologies? Provide an example of when you would use each.
2. How would you design a CI/CD pipeline for a cloud-based infrastructure (AWS)?
3. What are some common challenges you've faced while working with containerization (Docker)? How did you overcome them?
4. How do you ensure data consistency in a distributed database environment?
5. Can you explain the concept of microservices architecture? Provide an example of how you would implement it.
6. How do you handle conflicts in a team regarding coding style or architecture decisions?
7. Can you describe a situation where you had to troubleshoot a complex technical issue? How did you approach it?

**Scenario-Based Questions:**

1. Imagine you're leading a cross-functional team to develop a new software product. The team is struggling to meet the project timeline due to technical debt. What steps would you take to tackle this issue?
2. You're given a budget to upgrade the team's development environment. What would you prioritize, and why?
3. A critical issue is reported by a customer, and the team is overwhelmed with work. How would you allocate resources to resolve the issue quickly?
4. You're working with a team to release a new feature. The team is facing conflicts regarding the implementation. How would you facilitate a discussion to resolve the issue?
5. You're tasked with reducing the lead time of a development cycle by 30%. What steps would you take to achieve this goal?

**Behavioral Questions:**

1. Can you describe a situation where you had to communicate complex technical information to a non-technical stakeholder? How did you approach it?
2. Tell me about a time when you had to make a difficult decision regarding resource allocation. What factors did you consider, and what was the outcome?
3. Describe a situation where you had to adapt to a changing project scope or requirement. How did you manage the change?
4. Can you give an example of a successful project you led and your role in its success?
5. Tell me about a time when you received feedback on your leadership or communication style. How did you take it, and what changes did you make as a result?

These questions are designed to assess the candidate's technical skills, problem-solving abilities, leadership and communication skills, and ability to work under pressure. They also evaluate their adaptability, flexibility, and ability to make data-driven decisions.
